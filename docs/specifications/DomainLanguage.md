# HECE Domain Language

**Status:** Draft v1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the official vocabulary of HECE.

Every contributor should use these definitions consistently.

---

# Goal

A desired outcome provided by the user.

Example:

"Create fire using the human body."

---

# Goal Analysis

A structured representation of a Goal.

Generated by the Goal Interpreter.

Contains:

- domains
- keywords
- constraints
- complexity
- decomposition

---

# Constraint

Anything that limits possible solutions.

Examples:

- Physical laws
- Biological limitations
- Energy requirements
- Ethical restrictions

---

# Hypothesis

A possible solution that attempts to satisfy a Goal while respecting Constraints.

A hypothesis is NOT a fact.

---

# Evidence

Any scientific information supporting or contradicting a hypothesis.

Examples:

- Scientific paper
- Experimental result
- Mathematical proof
- Physical law

---

# Scientific Domain

A field of knowledge.

Examples:

- Physics
- Biology
- Chemistry
- Mathematics
- Computer Science

---

# Knowledge Graph

The structured network connecting concepts used by HECE.

---

# Simulation

A computational approximation of reality used to evaluate hypotheses.

---

# Reasoning

The process of transforming knowledge into conclusions.

---

# Exploration

The iterative search for new hypotheses.

---

# Plausibility

The degree to which a hypothesis is compatible with current scientific knowledge.

---

# Impossibility

A temporary classification indicating that current scientific knowledge cannot validate a hypothesis.

It must never be interpreted as absolute impossibility.

---

# Scientific Report

The final document generated by HECE containing:

- Goal
- Constraints
- Hypotheses
- Evidence
- Discussion
- Future Work
