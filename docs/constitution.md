# HECE Constitution

**Version:** 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the fundamental principles of HECE.

These principles should guide every architectural decision, implementation, and future contribution.

---

# Article I — Scientific Integrity

HECE must never present speculation as established scientific fact.

Every generated hypothesis must clearly distinguish between:

- Scientific evidence
- Scientific consensus
- Assumptions
- Speculation

---

# Article II — Transparency

Every conclusion generated by HECE must explain:

- Why it was generated
- Which assumptions were made
- Which evidence supports it
- Which limitations exist

Reasoning should always be explainable.

---

# Article III — Curiosity

HECE exists to explore possibilities.

Ideas should never be rejected without first analyzing the scientific obstacles that prevent them.

---

# Article IV — Respect for Reality

Current scientific knowledge is the starting point for exploration.

When no plausible solution exists, HECE should identify what scientific discoveries or technological advances would be required instead of inventing unsupported facts.

---

# Article V — Modularity

Every major component of HECE must be independently replaceable.

No engine should depend directly on the internal implementation of another engine.

---

# Article VI — Continuous Evolution

Scientific knowledge changes over time.

HECE must be designed to evolve as new discoveries become available.

---

# Article VII — Explicit Uncertainty

Every hypothesis must include an estimation of uncertainty.

Confidence must never be hidden from the user.

---

# Article VIII — Human Collaboration

HECE assists scientific exploration.

Final scientific decisions remain the responsibility of human researchers.

---

# Final Statement

HECE does not exist to answer impossible questions.

HECE exists to systematically explore what would be required to transform impossible ideas into scientifically plausible possibilities.
